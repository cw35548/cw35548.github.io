StyleMax

Overview

This is perhaps my most fully developed product and presents solid foundation of my second undergraduate degree. This project covers a lot of research in different technologies and education. I focus most on the project management and business development, Unity3D and AR programming, 200 pages game design proposal and basic UX concepts .

Purpose

It is a motion-sensing 3D game supporting middle school curriculum for biology class. We aim to make abstract knowledge interesting and understandable, and to make studying biology interesting for everyone.

Biggest challenge

Shortage of human resources and the lack of a dedicated technical person.

Lead a Team

Dealt with resource allocation, risk assessment, monitoring relationships between teammates and professors, conflict mitigation, effective time usage, progress tracking and understanding our BATNA (negotiation skills).

Solution

I become the lead developer responsible for the entire project and ended up learning Unity 3D. Another team member focused on 2D and 3D building. Other 3 group members assumed supporting positions and we maximized usage of time and resources.

Problem Solving
Unity 3D + Kinect
The most complicated part was using the gizmo in Unity3D to control the path of the camera. Also, many effects significantly affected the GPU performance and caused lag. We decided to use effects only if they are triggered.

Unity 3D + Kinect
We test the phisical collision, movements, gravity, speed, floating and water wave scales. In the real game, we only allow the system calls the corresponding animation while doing specific movements.
