G-commute

Shuttle Solution


for urban Seattle area

Overview

G-commute is a joint shuttle service designed for Seattlers’ daily commute. The goal is to bring a convenient, flexible and less traffic environment to all. In the end, we will create a win-win situation to benefit the company side, the employees and environmental impact.

Duration

This is a 3-month design thinking studio that addresses much on scoping & framing a problem. We've spent 2/3 of the time analyzing the insights of a problem by different research methods, summarizing the needs into design requirements, then go through the whole UX design process.

Biggest Challenge

Scaling the design question - we used a very specific design question in the beginning, then we modified it based on our new findings. We also spent much time on the flow of low-fi paper mockup, and since we couldn't decide the best solution, we decided to do the A/B testing to let users guide us.

Improvement

In the Lo-Fi mockup, the participants got confused by the buttons and the terms we used. We need to reduce the confusion like this. Also, instead of spreading all designs on the table, next time we will show only one screen during each process, so that they couldn't look at other screens to guess the "right" answer.

Initial Design Question: For people who work in a company with shuttle service in Seattle area and drive alone 30+ minutes back home in the weekdays, how can we encourage them to drive less by themselves?

Final Design Question: For professionals who work in Seattle area and drive alone 30+ minutes in the weekdays, how can we encourage them to drive less by themselves?

Research
Preliminary Research
Initial scope, market analysis & focus
We searched traffic congestion statistics in Seattle area from 2010 to 2017. There are 262,000 commuters in Seattle Downtown area, 25.4% of them drive alone to work. Even though the percentage of driving to work decreases year by year, the average commuting time for Seattle people increases dramatically every year.
Observe
Field Study
interview & observation
By affinity diagramming, we outlined major findings for both driving alone and busing participants. The purpose of grouping the data is to help us generate, organize and consolidate the information concerning the commuting process and the complex issues behind.
Data
Survey
29 valid response
Major findings in the result: Most people leave the company between 4pm to 6pm. Most people feel usually traffic in their daily commute. Most people choose driving due to the flexibility and time. Most people had considered taking public transportations but they gave up due to the time for waiting and the space limits.
Insights
Design Requirements
Triangulated Insights
We cross compared the research findings from secondary research, field study, and survey for listing the design requirements. Four keys: Heavier traffic while commuting back home; Negative feelings while driving alone; Unsatisfying shuttle service; Flexibility and necessity

In our ideation phase, we used personas, user scenarios, and storyboards to outline the identity of our users, tasks, and use cases.

We created two personas to represent two user groups working in companies with different culture and lifestyle. Based on the persona, we wrote scenarios describing under what kind of circumstances they will use our solution. At the very end, we put persona and user scenario together to visually represent where, why, and how they user our design.

Lo-Fi
Paper Mockup
rapid prototype & Address 6 Core Tasks
We firstly tried to quickly demonstrate our thoughts and findings to the users. At this stage, we especially focus on the user experience rather than caring about if it’s visual appealing. We address to test the 6 core tasks and aim to ensure the prototype meets the needs of our target users.
Findings
Usibility Testing
guide users to Think out loud
Our team spent about 10 minutes after each testing discussing our findings and 20 minutes to tidy our notes. Three different types of users provide opinions in very different aspects. It is especially helpful to ask them think out loud and let us know what they were hesitating about.
Refine
UX Flow
Refine the flow and the wireframe
we identified three main issues and modify the flow based on the following points: Simplify the main process to 2 steps only; Avoid spending time thinking the best way (we calculate for users); Different using habits between Android and Apple users. (Apple users do not familiar with the clock design)
UI
Hi-Fi Mockup
Informational Fidelity
Using the shuttle information as an example. We include all info in the lo-fi and reiterate based on the user research and usability study findings. The insights mainly contributed to cutting down the amount of information displayed on the interface, and further reduce the user’s cognitive load while using our design.
